H1: EAMCET (AP EAPCET) Exam Pattern, Syllabus & Important Dates — Andhra Pradesh Students' Guide
If you're preparing for EAMCET — now officially called AP EAPCET — getting clarity on the exam's structure is the first real step. This guide breaks it down simply, without the confusing jargon found on most exam portals.

H2: Understanding the Exam Pattern
H3: Format and Duration
The exam is a computer-based test consisting of 160 multiple-choice questions, each worth one mark, to be completed within three hours. There is no negative marking, so attempting every question — even with an educated guess — works in your favor.
H3: Stream-Wise Subject Split
Engineering aspirants are tested on Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics. Agriculture and Pharmacy aspirants are tested on Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. Some students choose to appear for both streams by submitting separate applications.

H2: Syllabus Structure
H3: Based Entirely on Intermediate Curriculum
The good news for Andhra Pradesh students is that the syllabus mirrors the Class 11 and Class 12 curriculum set by the Board of Intermediate Education. There's no separate, unfamiliar content to learn from scratch.
H3: Where to Focus Your Energy
Within this syllabus, certain chapters — like Calculus and Mechanics — consistently carry more weight than others. Structuring your revision around topic-wise importance, rather than treating every chapter equally, leads to smarter preparation.

H2: Key Milestones to Track Through the Year
H3: Notification and Registration
The official notification, along with registration details, is released by APSCHE — typically a few months before the exam. It's worth tracking this on the official portal rather than relying solely on secondhand information.
H3: Hall Ticket and Exam Window
Hall tickets are usually released shortly before the exam, and the exam itself is conducted across a few consecutive days for the engineering stream, followed by separate dates for agriculture and pharmacy.
H3: Results and Counselling
Results and rank cards typically follow within a few weeks of the exam, after which the counselling process — including document verification and seat allotment — begins.
